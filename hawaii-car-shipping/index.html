General FAQ

What documents do I need to provide for a PCS vehicle transport to or from Hawaii?

You will need to provide a copy of your military orders, a valid ID, proof of vehicle ownership (registration and title), and a power of attorney if someone else is handling the vehicle on your behalf.

How far in advance should I schedule my vehicle transport for a PCS move to Hawaii?

It’s recommended to schedule your vehicle transport at least 4-6 weeks in advance to ensure availability and allow for any necessary arrangements regarding shipping dates and port processing.

Are there any specific preparation steps I need to take for my vehicle before transport to Hawaii?

Yes, ensure the vehicle is clean, has no personal belongings inside, only has about a quarter tank of fuel, and that any loose parts are secured or removed. Additionally, document the vehicle’s condition with photos for reference.

What are the restrictions or limitations on transporting personal items in my vehicle during a PCS to Hawaii?

Generally, personal items are not allowed inside the vehicle during transport due to weight restrictions and security concerns. Any items in the vehicle may be subject to removal at the port.

How will I be notified about the arrival of my vehicle at the port in Hawaii?

You will receive notifications via email and a phone call from the transport service as your vehicle nears its destination, giving you details about the pickup time and location.

What happens if my vehicle is damaged during transport to or from Hawaii?

In the unlikely event of damage, inspect your vehicle at the port before signing the Bill of Lading.

Can I track the transport of my vehicle during my PCS move to Hawaii?

Yes, most transport services offer tracking solutions that allow you to monitor the progress of your vehicle online or through customer service updates.

What is the process for picking up my vehicle at the port in Hawaii?

Upon arrival, go to the designated port with your ID and necessary documents. Conduct a thorough inspection with a port agent, sign the Bill of Lading after confirming the vehicle’s condition, and then you can take possession of your vehicle.

Are there any additional fees or charges I should expect for vehicle transport during my PCS to Hawaii?

Typically, the quoted transport price should include all necessary fees. However, always verify if there are additional charges for port handling, customs clearance, or other administrative fees.

What should I do if my PCS dates change after I've already scheduled vehicle transport to or from Hawaii?

Contact your transport provider as soon as possible to inform them of the date changes. Most companies are flexible and can adjust the shipping schedule to accommodate your new dates, though it may involve additional fees depending on the timing and availability.

Insurance & Personal Items

Does my vehicle need to be insured to be shipped?

While it’s recommended, your vehicle doesn’t need to be insured for shipping. During trucking, your vehicle is covered by the carrier’s cargo and liability insurance policies up to $100,000.  However, Cargo liability insurance, does not cover Acts of God, such as hail, tornados, hurricanes, etc., and therefore it is recommended that you maintain coverage on your vehicle that would cover damage occurring due to an Act of God.

For Overseas shipments such as for Hawaii and Guam, we strongly suggest you take out Marine Insurance Coverage because your vehicle does not have any coverage.  Transcar offers Marine Insurance so if you’re interested in this coverage, please contact your sales representative.

Is my vehicle insured during transport?

  • Land Shipments: Yes, your vehicle is covered by the carrier’s Trucking Cargo Insurance policy, typically up to $100,000, subject to certain limitations and exclusions.
  • Ocean Shipments: No, your vehicle is not insured.  Transcar does offer Marine Insurance which is a All Risk policy.  Please contact your sales representative for details. 

Do I have to pay a deductible?

  • For Land Shipments: No, there is no deductible to pay if you need to file a claim for damage during transport.
  • For Oceans Shipments: If you take out Transcar’s Marine Insurance, there is a 2% deductible.

Can I ship personal items in my car?

No, Personal items are not allowed to be left in the vehicle.  Seats must remain upright. Be aware that carriers have restrictions, and unauthorized items may lead to additional fees or refusal of shipment.

Auto carriers are not licensed to carry household goods or personal items, therefore it is not recommended to leave items of that nature in your vehicle. Factory issued equipment and aftermarket items permanently installed are allowed to be left with the vehicle and will be noted accordingly on the truck bill of lading. If you do leave items in the trunk of your vehicle such as pillows, blankets, etc., the cargo insurance liability or driver will not accept any liability in case of loss or damage. Therefore, it is not recommended to leave anything in your vehicle other than standard factory issued equipment and accessories.

Are personal items in my car insured?

No, personal belongings inside your vehicle are not covered by the carrier’s insurance. Neither Transcar nor the carrier is liable for any personal items left in the vehicle during transport.

What if my personal items damage my car during transport?

Any damage to your vehicle’s interior caused by personal items is not covered by the carrier’s insurance. Personal items can shift during transit, potentially causing damage, so pack carefully.

How is the value of my car determined for insurance purposes?

The value is based on the actual cash value of your vehicle at the time of transport.

What should I do if my car is damaged during transport?

  1. Inspect your vehicle thoroughly upon delivery.
  2. Note any damages on the Bill of Lading/Vehicle Condition Report.
  3. Obtain a copy of the signed Bill of Lading from the driver.
  4. Take photographs of the damage.
  5. Contact Transcar to request the carrier’s Certificate of Insurance.
  6. File a claim with the carrier’s insurance company.

Do I need to be present at pickup or delivery?

Yes, you or an authorized representative (over 18 years old) must be present at both pickup and delivery to inspect the vehicle and sign the Bill of Lading.

What is a Bill of Lading?

The Bill of Lading (BOL) is a legal document between you and the carrier. It outlines the details of the shipment, serves as a receipt, and records the condition of your vehicle at pickup and delivery. It’s crucial for filing any insurance claims.

What is the procedure for pickup and delivery?

At Pickup:

  • Inspect your vehicle with the driver.
  • Note any existing damages on the BOL.
  • Both parties sign the BOL.
  • Receive a copy for your records.

At Delivery:

  • Inspect your vehicle for any new damages.
  • Note any new damages on the BOL.
  • Both parties sign the BOL.
  • Pay any remaining balance if applicable.

Do I need to drop off or pick up my vehicle anywhere?

No, Transcar offers door-to-door service. The driver will pick up and deliver your vehicle as close to your specified addresses as safely and legally possible. In some cases, due to restrictions, you may need to meet the driver at a nearby location.

How should I prepare my car for shipment?

  • Clean your vehicle to allow for a thorough inspection.
  • Remove personal items, especially valuables.
  • Disable alarms and remove toll tags.
  • Ensure only a quarter tank of gas is in the vehicle.
  • Check for leaks and mechanical issues.
  • Secure or remove loose parts like antennas or spoilers.

How much gas should I leave in my car?

We recommend leaving between 1/8 and 1/4 tank of fuel. This is sufficient for loading and unloading.

Do I need a vehicle title or registration to ship it?

For trucking only shipments: No, only the vehicle and keys are required. However, ensure the person releasing and receiving the vehicle has proper authorization.

For ocean shipments: Yes, depending on where it’s going, we will need these.  Please consult with our sales representative for exact documents required.

Can I get a quote if I don't know the exact address?

Yes, providing the city or zip code is sufficient for an accurate quote. Exact addresses can be updated later.

What does happen when I ship my car?

When you ship your car with us, you have given us a date that your car will be available for shipping. Our dispatchers will then work to assign your automobile to the first available truck that can pick it up after it is ready for shipment. Our customer service department will then advise both the pickup and delivery points of contact that you have provided to us, with the estimated pick up and delivery dates. Our driver will then contact you or your appointed representatives at origin and destination, to further advise his estimated time of arrival at your location and also to coordinate a safe and legal loading or unloading location at or near your residence. At the time of pick up, the driver will complete a joint inspection of your auto and also at time of delivery. This is done in order to assure that your car has arrived safely and without transport damages. A copy of this inspection will be provided at time of pick up and at time of final delivery.

Will my vehicle be driven?

No your vehicle will NOT be driven other than for loading onto the transporter truck and off loading from the transporter.  The odometer readings should be noted at pickup and delivery of the vehicle. Any discrepancies will be noted and signed off by the driver on the delivery receipt.

How do I prepare my car for pickup?

Any loose parts or specialty items such as spoilers, fog lights, or mirrors should be removed or secured properly for  transport. Antennas should be removed or retracted. There should be an adequate supply of antifreeze in the radiator. The gasoline tank should have no more than one quarter. More gasoline in the tank only adds weight for transport. Make sure the battery is secure, with no leaks and fluid levels low enough to allow for expansion during transit. Any tears in convertible tops should be sealed or repaired to avoid further damage by air during transit.  Tearing of convertible tops by wind will not be covered by cargo insurance.  Alarm devices should be deactivated. Lastly, make sure that the vehicle is clean enough to allow accurate inspection at pickup.

What is the difference between open and enclosed transport?

  • Open Transport: Your vehicle is transported on an open carrier, exposed to weather and road conditions. This is the most common and cost-effective method.
  • Enclosed Transport: Your vehicle is shipped in a fully enclosed trailer, protecting it from the elements and road debris. Ideal for luxury, classic, or high-value vehicles.

What is door-to-door auto transport?

What is the difference between a Carrier and a Broker?

A carrier is a company that owns and operates the trucks used to transport vehicles. Often, carriers are owner-operators who manage their own schedules and typically serve specific routes. They usually coordinate shipments through brokers to find customers.

A broker, like Transcar, maintains a vast network of carriers across the country. Brokers can often provide lower rates because they have access to multiple carriers competing for your shipment. They also handle the verification of carriers’ USDOT authority, federal licenses, and cargo insurance, ensuring that your vehicle is transported by a legitimate and qualified carrier. This vetting process can be challenging to manage on your own, so working with a broker simplifies the process and provides peace of mind.
