Many of the preparation requirements are similar to those for your government-shipped vehicle, which you may already be familiar with. Preparing your POV
﻿for Shipment

  • The vehicle’s interior and exterior must be cleaned thoroughly
  • Ensure there’s less than ¼ of fuel in the tank – should not be above that
  • Make sure you have the spare tire (2 max) and the jack and lug wrench
  • The vehicle should only have factory-installed items with no personal items
  • The vehicle should only have tools that it came with from the factory (no toolboxes)
  • The vehicle should only have auto manufacturer-installed roof racks
  • The vehicle should not have tailgate covers, nets, or bras
  • The vehicle should not have extra speakers or equalizers if not bolted
  • No vehicle decorative elements or ornaments are allowed
  • Ensure you remove all seat covers
  • It’s also recommended that you remove the license plates
  • All keys to all vehicle compartments must be provided, including tire locks (All vehicle compartments are inspected by the Department of Homeland Security – any vehicle without access to locked compartments will be detained.)
  • You must also disengage the vehicle’s alarm system before shipping if possible
  • 1 copy of Power of Attorney is required if other than that of the Spouse of Service Member

Frequently Asked Questions

Welcome to Transcar’s comprehensive FAQ section. We have organized the most common
questions into categories to help you find the information you need quickly and efficiently.

What documents do I need to provide for a PCS vehicle transport to or from Guam?

You will need to provide a copy of your military orders, a valid ID, proof of vehicle ownership (registration and title), and a power of attorney if someone else is handling the vehicle on your behalf.

How far in advance should I schedule my vehicle transport for a PCS move to Guam?

It’s recommended to schedule your vehicle transport at least 4-6 weeks in advance to ensure availability and allow for any necessary arrangements regarding shipping dates and port processing.

Are there any specific preparation steps I need to take for my vehicle before transport to Guam?

Yes, ensure the vehicle is clean, has no personal belongings inside, only has about a quarter tank of fuel, and that any loose parts are secured or removed. Additionally, document the vehicle’s condition with photos for reference.

What are the restrictions or limitations on transporting personal items in my vehicle during a PCS to Guam?

Generally, personal items are not allowed inside the vehicle during transport due to weight restrictions and security concerns. Any items in the vehicle may be subject to removal at the port.

How will I be notified about the arrival of my vehicle at the port in Guam?

You will receive notifications via email and a phone call from the transport service as your vehicle nears its destination, giving you details about the pickup time and location.

What happens if my vehicle is damaged during transport to or from Guam?

In the unlikely event of damage, inspect your vehicle at the port before signing the Bill of Lading.

Can I track the transport of my vehicle during my PCS move to Guam?

Yes, most transport services offer tracking solutions that allow you to monitor the progress of your vehicle online or through customer service updates.

What is the process for picking up my vehicle at the port in Guam?

Upon arrival, go to the designated port with your ID and necessary documents. Conduct a thorough inspection with a port agent, sign the Bill of Lading after confirming the vehicle’s condition, and then you can take possession of your vehicle.

Are there any additional fees or charges I should expect for vehicle transport during my PCS to Guam?

Typically, the quoted transport price should include all necessary fees. However, always verify if there are additional charges for port handling, customs clearance, or other administrative fees.

What should I do if my PCS dates change after I've already scheduled vehicle transport to or from Guam?

Contact your transport provider as soon as possible to inform them of the date changes. Most companies are flexible and can adjust the shipping schedule to accommodate your new dates, though it may involve additional fees depending on the timing and availability.
